Full Course Header (Verbatim)

Original course framing and alignment statements

⌄
 
**PED6 / PROFED03 / EDUC 111: The Child and Adolescent Learners and Learning Principles – Ultra-Comprehensive, In-Depth, Exhaustive Discussion (Philippine BEEd/BSEd Context, Aligned with CHED CMO 30 s. 2017, PPST, DepEd MATATAG, RA 10533 & RA 11650)**

This foundational 3-unit course (54 hours, typically 18 weeks) is mandatory in all Philippine teacher-education programs. It equips pre-service teachers with research-based mastery of biological, linguistic, cognitive, social-emotional, and moral dimensions of development from birth to 18/19 years. It emphasizes **learner-centered** principles, inclusive practices, and direct application to K-12 (especially Grades 1–10 for elementary, 7–12 for secondary).  

The course directly supports **Philippine Professional Standards for Teachers (PPST, DepEd Order 42 s. 2017)**:  
- Domain 1 (Content Knowledge & Pedagogy)  
- Domain 2 (Learning Environment)  
- Domain 3 (Diversity of Learners)  
- Domain 4 (Curriculum & Planning)  
- Domain 5 (Assessment & Reporting)  

It prepares teachers for DepEd’s **MATATAG** curriculum, Mother-Tongue-Based Multilingual Education (MTB-MLE), inclusive education, positive discipline (DepEd Order 40 s. 2012), and 21st-century skills. Assessment usually includes quizzes, reaction papers, case studies, lesson plans applying theories, and a final portfolio or developmental case analysis of a real Filipino child/adolescent.

Below is **every aspect** expanded with greater depth, exact theories, full lists, criticisms, Philippine examples, classroom applications, and visual aids.

Fundamental Concepts: Growth, Development, Maturation, Learning & Readiness

Key definitions + principles of human development + approaches to studying development

⌄
 
### 1. Fundamental Concepts: Growth, Development, Maturation, Learning & Readiness
**Growth** – Quantitative, measurable changes (e.g., height, weight, brain volume). Follows cephalocaudal (head-to-toe) and proximodistal (center-to-periphery) patterns. Influenced by nutrition, genetics, and environment. Philippine context: Stunting affects ~30% of children under 5 (FNRI data); teachers must monitor and refer for feeding programs.

**Development** – Qualitative, progressive changes in complexity, function, and organization. Multidimensional (physical, cognitive, language, socio-emotional, moral, spiritual) and multidirectional. Continues across the lifespan.

**Maturation** – Genetically timed unfolding (e.g., walking at ~12 months, puberty at 10–14). Provides “readiness” but requires stimulation. Gesell’s maturationist view: Development is largely innate; environment plays minor role (criticized as too deterministic).

**Learning** – Relatively permanent change in behavior, knowledge, or skill from experience, interaction, or instruction. Not the same as maturation (e.g., a 5-year-old can be taught to read only when maturationally ready).

**Key Principles of Human Development** (synthesized from all major sources; 12 most cited):
1. Development is **orderly and sequential** (predictable stages).
2. **Cephalocaudal & proximodistal** gradients.
3. Proceeds at **different rates** (asynchronous – e.g., cognitive spurts in adolescence).
4. **Continuous yet staged** (quantitative + qualitative shifts).
5. **Interaction of heredity (nature) and environment (nurture)** – epigenetics: genes can be expressed differently based on experiences.
6. **Critical/sensitive periods** (e.g., language 0–7 years; attachment 0–3 years).
7. **Holistic/interrelated** – physical health affects cognition (e.g., iron deficiency → poor attention).
8. **Plasticity** – brain and behavior can be modified (greatest in early years).
9. **Individual differences** – influenced by genetics, culture, SES.
10. **Contextual** (Bronfenbrenner) – development in nested systems.
11. **Cumulative** – early experiences profoundly shape later outcomes.
12. **Lifelong** – no decline assumed; growth possible at any age.

**Approaches to Studying Development**:
- Traditional (focus 0–18; decline after).
- Lifespan perspective (Baltes): Multidimensional, plastic, contextual, historical.

### 2. Developmental Tasks – Robert Havighurst (Full Detailed List)
Tasks arise at specific ages from biological, psychological, and societal pressures. Successful mastery → happiness & later success; failure → unhappiness & difficulty with next tasks.



**Infancy & Early Childhood (0–5 years)**  
- Learning to walk, eat solid foods, talk, control elimination.  
- Learning sex differences & modesty.  
- Forming concepts & language to describe reality.  
- Getting ready to read.  
- Distinguishing right/wrong & developing conscience.

**Middle Childhood (6–12 years)**  
- Learning physical skills for ordinary games.  
- Building wholesome self-attitudes.  
- Learning to get along with age-mates.  
- Learning appropriate masculine/feminine social roles.  
- Developing reading, writing, calculating skills.  
- Developing concepts for everyday living.  
- Developing conscience, morality, values.  
- Achieving personal independence.  
- Developing attitudes toward social groups/institutions.

**Adolescence (13–18 years)**  
- Achieving mature relations with both sexes.  
- Achieving masculine/feminine social role.  
- Accepting physique & using body effectively.  
- Achieving emotional independence from parents/adults.  
- Preparing for marriage & family life.  
- Preparing for economic career.  
- Acquiring values & ethical system.  
- Desiring & achieving socially responsible behavior.

Philippine application: In rural areas, adolescents may take on early economic roles (farming, vending) – teachers must support career guidance aligned with this.

### 3. Major Theories of Development (Expanded with Full Stages, Criticisms, Applications)

**Jean Piaget – Cognitive Development (Constructivist)**  
Children actively construct knowledge via assimilation, accommodation, equilibration.





**Erik Erikson – Psychosocial Development (8 stages, lifespan focus on first 5–6)**  
Each stage: crisis → virtue if resolved positively.







**Lev Vygotsky – Socio-Cultural Theory**  
Social interaction drives development. Key concepts: Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD), More Knowledgeable Other (MKO), scaffolding, private speech, cultural tools (language, symbols). Philippine fit: Bayanihan, group work, elder respect.

**Urie Bronfenbrenner – Bioecological Systems Theory**  
Development in nested, interacting systems.



**Lawrence Kohlberg – Moral Development (Cognitive-Moral)**  
6 stages in 3 levels; based on justice reasoning (Heinz dilemma).





**Additional Theories Commonly Covered**  
- **Freud (Psychosexual)**: Oral, Anal, Phallic, Latency, Genital – early experiences shape personality (criticized: untestable, sexist).  
- **Bandura (Social Cognitive)**: Observational learning, modeling, self-efficacy, reciprocal determinism.  
- **Bowlby/Ainsworth (Attachment)**: Secure, Avoidant, Ambivalent, Disorganized – affects all later relationships.  
- **Gardner (Multiple Intelligences, 1983/1999)**: 8–9 intelligences (linguistic, logical-mathematical, spatial, musical, bodily-kinesthetic, interpersonal, intrapersonal, naturalistic, existential). Teachers must differentiate.



**Goleman Emotional Intelligence**: Self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship management – crucial for adolescent classroom management.

The 14 Learner-Centered Psychological Principles (APA, 1997) – Core of the Course

Cognitive/metacognitive, motivational/affective, developmental/social, individual differences

⌄
 
### 4. The 14 Learner-Centered Psychological Principles (APA, 1997) – Core of the Course (Full Detailed List)







**Cognitive & Metacognitive Factors (6)**  
1. **Nature of the learning process**: Learning complex material is intentional construction of meaning from information/experience.  
2. **Goals of the learning process**: Successful learners create coherent representations; they are goal-directed.  
3. **Construction of knowledge**: Learners link new info to existing knowledge.  
4. **Strategic thinking & reasoning**: Learners use strategies for thinking/reasoning.  
5. **Thinking about thinking (metacognition)**: Learners monitor/regulate their own cognition.  
6. **Context of learning**: Learning influenced by environmental factors (culture, technology, instruction).

**Motivational & Affective Factors (3)**  
7. **Motivational & emotional influences**: Thoughts, beliefs, emotions enhance or interfere with learning.  
8. **Intrinsic motivation**: Novelty, relevance, choice, control promote intrinsic motivation.  
9. **Effects of motivation on effort**: Learners invest effort when motivated for challenging tasks.

**Developmental & Social Factors (2)**  
10. **Developmental influences**: Learning is most effective when aligned with developmental level.  
11. **Social influences**: Social interactions shape learning (cooperative, competitive, collaborative).

**Individual Differences Factors (3)**  
12. **Individual differences**: Learners differ in strategies, approaches, capabilities, preferences.  
13. **Learning & diversity**: Learning influenced by linguistic, cultural, gender differences.  
14. **Standards & assessment**: Assessment must reflect learning goals and provide feedback for progress.

**Classroom Applications (Philippine examples)**: Use group work (bayanihan), reflection journals (metacognition), choice boards for MI, culturally relevant examples (Filipino heroes, local problems).

Learning Theories & Processes (Expanded)

Behavioral, cognitive, constructivist, humanistic, social learning, connectivism

⌄
 
### 5. Learning Theories & Processes (Expanded)
- **Behavioral**: Thorndike (law of effect), Pavlov (classical), Skinner (operant – positive/negative reinforcement, punishment, extinction). Applications: Token economy, praise.  
- **Cognitive/Information Processing**: Attention → Sensory → Short-term/working → Long-term memory (rehearsal, encoding, retrieval). Metacognition.  
- **Constructivist**: Piaget + Vygotsky extensions; discovery learning, scaffolding, authentic tasks.  
- **Humanistic (Rogers)**: Learner-centered, unconditional positive regard, freedom to learn.  
- **Social Learning (Bandura)**: Modeling, vicarious reinforcement.  
- **Connectivism (Siemens)**: Learning in digital networks (relevant for Gen Z/Alpha in PH).

Factors Influencing Development & Learning

Biological, environmental/socio-cultural, exceptionalities + UDL

⌄
 
### 6. Factors Influencing Development & Learning
**Biological**: Genetics, nutrition, health, brain development (synaptogenesis 0–3, pruning, adolescent prefrontal lag → risk-taking, emotion over logic).  
**Environmental/Socio-cultural**: Family (extended in PH), SES (poverty affects 1 in 4 Filipino children), peers, school, media, culture (collectivism, religiosity). OFW parents, typhoons/disasters (resilience needed). Multilingualism (positive for cognition).  
**Exceptionalities**: Gifted, disabilities (IDEA categories adapted in PH SPED), ADHD, autism – teachers must apply Universal Design for Learning (UDL).

Motivation, Classroom Management, Instruction & Assessment

Self-Determination, ARCS, Expectancy-Value + preventive/supportive/corrective + assessment types

⌄
 
### 7. Motivation, Classroom Management, Instruction & Assessment
**Motivation Theories**: Self-Determination (autonomy, competence, relatedness), ARCS (Attention, Relevance, Confidence, Satisfaction), Expectancy-Value. Strategies: Real-life problems (e.g., climate change in PH), growth mindset praise.  
**Management**: Preventive (clear rules, routines), supportive (positive relationships), corrective (restorative justice over punishment).  
**Instruction**: Differentiated (Tomlinson), project-based, inquiry, technology integration.  
**Assessment**: Formative (daily checks), summative, authentic (portfolios, performances), rubrics. Avoid bias against indigenous/minority learners.
